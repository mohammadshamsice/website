About
Education & Background

I am a M.Sc. student in Structural Engineering at Sharif University of Technology, one of the most prestigious technical universities in the Middle East. My research focuses on developing Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) that integrate finite element simulations with real sensor data for structural health monitoring and digital twin development.

Prior to Sharif, I earned my B.Sc. in Civil Engineering from Shahid Bahonar University of Kerman, where I ranked 2nd among 66 peers and earned a bronze medal at the Iran National Civil Engineering Olympiad. I am passionate about bridging the gap between computational mechanics and data-driven methods.

I am actively seeking PhD positions for Fall 2026 in structural engineering, computational mechanics, or structural health monitoring, where I can contribute to advancing next-generation intelligent structural systems.

Selected Projects
Fall 2025 — Graduate Course Project
Random Vibration of Structures
Experimental modal identification and stochastic response analysis of MDOF systems and base-isolated structures.
Python PSD/FRF Modal Analysis Base Isolation Stochastic Processes
  • Experimental modal identification of multi-degree-of-freedom structural systems under sine-sweep and random excitation.
  • Estimation of natural frequencies, damping ratios, mode shapes, stationarity, and ergodicity of stochastic excitation records.
  • Frequency-domain and time-domain analysis of structural response under impact and random loading using PSD and FRF methods.
  • Stochastic response analysis of fixed-base and base-isolated (HDRB/LRB) RC frame structures subjected to white-noise and spectrum-compatible earthquake excitations.
  • Computation of response statistics, spectral density functions, impulse response functions, and soil-filtered seismic inputs using Python.

Research
M.Sc. Thesis
Hybrid Physics-Informed Neural Networks Integrating Finite Element and Sensor Data for Structural Health Monitoring and Digital Twin Development
Supervisor: Dr. Ali Bakhshi Sharif University of Technology Ongoing

This thesis develops a novel framework that combines physics-informed neural networks with finite element models and real sensor measurements to create accurate digital twins of structures. The approach enables real-time structural health monitoring with physically consistent predictions, even under limited sensor configurations.

  • Development of Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) for structural response prediction and damage detection
  • Integration of finite element simulations and sensor measurements for digital twin development
  • Application of machine learning and structural dynamics methods for Structural Health Monitoring
  • Validation using experimental data and benchmark structural systems
